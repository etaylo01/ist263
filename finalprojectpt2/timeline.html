The Timeline of Artificial Intelligence

1950

Turning suggested that since humans can use information to solve problems, can’t computers too?

  • Turning wrote Computing Machinery and Intelligence
1956

Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence

  • Hosted by John McCarthy and Marvin Minsky
  • “McCarthy, imagining a great collaborative effort, brought together top researchers from various fields for an open ended discussion on artificial intelligence, the term which he coined at the very event” Link
  • Later that year, Allen Newell, J.C. Shaw, and Herbert Simon create the Logic Theorist, the first-ever running AI software program. Link
1956-1974

AI flourishing

  • Before 1949, computers could not store information, but now computers were cheaper, faster, and could store information!
  • Early demonstrations such as Newell and Simon’s General Problem Solver and Joseph Weizenbaum’s ELIZA showed promise toward the goals of problem solving and the interpretation of spoken language respectively. Link
  • Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA) funded AI research
1980's

Emergence of Deep Learning

  • “John Hopfield and David Rumelhart popularized “deep learning” techniques which allowed computers to learn using experience” Link
  • Neural Networks become widely used in AI applications Link
1982-1990

Investing in AI

  • Japanese Government invests “$400 million dollars with the goals of revolutionizing computer processing, implementing logic programming, and improving artificial intelligence.” Link
1997

Outsmarting Humans

  • World chess champion and grand master Gary Kasparov was defeated by IBM’s Deep Blue, a chess computer program.
1995

Learning AI

  • Stuart Russell and Peter Novig publishes one of the leading textbooks in the study of AI: Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach. They describe four potential goals or definitions of AI.
  • “Human approach:
    • Systems that think like humans
    • Systems that act like humans
  • Ideal approach:
    • Systems that think rationally
    • Systems that act rationally” Link
2015

AI and Images

  • “Baidu's Minwa supercomputer uses a special kind of deep neural network called a convolutional neural network to identify and categorize images with a higher rate of accuracy than the average human” Link
2023

Rise in language models

  • Programs such as ChatGPT created; causing enormous change in performance of AI and its potential to drive enterprise value.
  • Continued development of deep-learning models
